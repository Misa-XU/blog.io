Norm

Posted on 2020-08-03 Edited on 2020-08-07 In Math , Linear Algebra

In mathematics, a norm is a function from a vector space over the real or complex numbers to the nonnegative real numbers that satisfies certain properties pertaining to scalability and additivity, and takes the value zero only if the input vector is zero.

The Euclidean norm or 2-norm is a specific norm on a Euclidean vector space, that is strongly related to the Euclidean distance, and equals the square root of the inner product of a vector with itself.
